LA SICILIA

Sicilia, beautiful Sicilia!

The largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, Sicily has a rich and diverse history, with an identity unique to the rest of Italy.

Originally settled by the native peoples of Italy--through colonization Sicily became home to a majority Greek population, founding large settlements like Syracuse.

Dominion over Sicily was the cause of the First and Second Punic Wars fought between the Roman republic and Carthage--after Roman victory, Sicily became the first Roman province outside of the Italian peninsula.

With the fall of the Western Roman Empire, possession of the island changed hands frequently: from Germanic tribes, to the Greek Byzantines, to the Arabs of North Africa, and finally to the Normans, European knights descended from Vikings.

The conquering Normans embraced many of the traditions of the Sicilian people, by now a certified melting pot of Greek, Muslim and Italian cultures; and the Norman Kingdom of Sicily grew to be one of the richest in Europe.

Through the complicated machinations of European monarchies (x married y, the kingdom was inherited by z) the Kingdom of Sicily became part of Spain, was lost in the Napoleonic Wars, and then joined with the Kingdom of Naples to form The Kingdom of Two Sicilies, with a French king on the throne.

There were many revolts against the rule of the French Bourbons, and when the process of Italian unification began, a plebiscite vote was held in which 75% of the population of the Sicilian kingdom chose to unify with the new Italian state.

Upon unification, the Sicilian economy and development lagged behind that of northern Italy, and continues to do so to this day.

Due to economic mismanagement of the island, Sicily has the highest rates of emigration and expatriation in Italy--the Sicilian diaspora is said to number over 10,000,000 globally, and many beloved Italian-American celebrities are of Sicilian descent:

  • Frank Sinatra
  • Steve Buscemi
  • Ariana Grande
  • Al Pacino
  • Louis Prima
  • Martin Scorsese
  • Vinny from Jersey Shore
  • Me, Gino. From Gino’s Sicilian Pizzeria


Salute, to this beautiful island with beautiful history and beautiful people!
